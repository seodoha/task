TSCA Toxic Substance Control Act
  • Applicable law
    Toxic Substance Control Act
  • Effective date
    October 11, 1976
  • Purpose
    Ensuring that chemicals and mixtures do not pose unreasonable risks to human health or the environment
  • Excluded substances
    The products below are exempt from TSCA because they are already regulated by other laws at the time the TSCA came into force
    • Pesticide
    • Cigarettes and tobacco products
    • Radioactive substances
    • Weapons and ammunition
    • Food, food additives, pharmaceuticals and cosmetics
Phase-in
substances list
TSCA Inventory
  • As of 2019, about 84,000 substances listed (Confidential list of around 16,000 substances)
  • US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) has made the Phase-in substance List available for download online
    View details of TSCA Inventory
  • Substances not listed on Phase-in list are considered non-phase-in substances
Classification of Phase-in substances
  • Active
    Substance
    Phase-in substances reported activity (manufacture/import) in the US since 2006
    Manufacture/import possible without separate notice
  • Inactive
    Substance
    Phase-in substances not reported activity (manufacture/import) in the US since 2006
    Submit a ‘forward-looking notice (Form-B Notice)’ at least 90 days prior to manufacturing/import
Pre-Manufacture
Notice (PMN)
PMN Pre-Manufacture Notice
  • Non-phase-in substances not listed in TSCA Inventory need to get PMN approved by EPA before manufacturing/importing
  • PMN submission by: US manufacturer/importer
  • PMN submission info
    • Information of manufacturer/importer
    • Substance identification information: CASrN, structural formula, impurities/by-products, MSDS, etc.
    • Retained physical/chemical properties, human and environmental toxicity data
    • Expected volume of manufacturing/importing for three years after notice
    • Usage and number of sub-users
    • Manufacturing and use flow chart / Exposure information to workers and the environment
