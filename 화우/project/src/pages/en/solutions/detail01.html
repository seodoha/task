Business Introduction

Drawing on combined experience and expertise of more than 50 specialists in antitrust and competition issues, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice provides clients with a cutting-edge expertise in all aspects of Korean antitrust and competition laws, including the Monopoly Regulation and Fair Trade Act, the Fair Transactions in Subcontracting Act, the Act on the Regulation of Terms and Conditions, the Act on Fair Labeling and Advertising, the Fair Transactions in Franchise Business Act, the Act on Fair Transactions in Large Retail Business, the Act on Door-to-Door Sales, the Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, the Installment Transactions Act, and the Fair Agency Transactions Act. The Practice offers one-stop services covering regulatory, litigation and investigations matters.

As a long-standing advisor which excels in all areas of antitrust and competition issues faced by global multinational corporations as well as major domestic companies, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice has been acclaimed not only domestically but also internationally. It has been chosen as an Elite Law Firm in competition law for 14 consecutive years from 2008 to 2021 by Global Competition Review (GCR), a globally-recognized competition law journal.

The Practice collaborates with experts from the firm’s other practices, including intellectual property, finance, corporate and tax, and provides clients with seamless services specific to a wide spectrum of needs and industries. The Practice has been playing a central role in industry-changing antitrust matters, in particular in IT, finance, logistics, and platform industries.

Key Business Areas

  • Cartel
    Investigations by the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) are frequent on cartel activities, and the results of the investigations have a huge impact on companies. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice has an excellent track record in defending clients throughout the entire process of cartel investigations by the KFTC and related litigation. The Practice also been accumulating outstanding accomplishments in representing clients in multi-jurisdictional cartel cases by working closely with foreign law firms.
  • Merger Filings
    With respect to corporate M&A transactions which are effected in various ways, including share acquisitions, mergers, overlapping officers and directors, business transfers, or participation in the establishment of new companies, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice advises clients at every stage of the merger process, including structuring of transactions, preparation of merger filings and obtainment of the KFTC's approval, and representation in appeal proceedings to challenge corrective orders issued by the KFTC. The Practice provides clients with seamless services to close M&A transactions within desired timelines based on the collaboration of our attorneys with in-house economists and expert advisors who were former KFTC officers.
  • Abuse of Market Dominance
    Abuse of market dominance gives rise to some of the most complex issues in antitrust and competition law. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice offers creative and effective solutions to these issues, collaborating with world-renowned competition law experts and economists. The Practice is building a leading edge in the field based on the latest legal knowledge and expertise as well as sophisticated industry insight.
  • Unfair Trade Practices
    Under the Monopoly Regulations and Fair Trade Act, unfair trade practices include refusal to deal, discriminatory treatment, exclusion of competitors, unfair customer inducement, coercion of trade, abuse of superior bargaining position, trading on restrictive conditions, and interference with business activities. The recent trends of the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) show strengthened law enforcement against unfair trade practices of online platform operators in the digital economy sector. Based on its preemptive understanding of the rapidly changing market environment and regulatory trends, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice offers prompt and practical solutions that meet the diverse needs of clients.
  • Unfair Affiliate Transactions
    Since the inception of the Business Group Bureau, the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) has strengthened regulations on affiliate transactions with respect to medium-sized enterprises as well as large companies. In particular, the KFTC is increasingly filing criminal charges against individuals, including senior officers and shareholders, who have directed or involved in affiliate transactions. Reflecting such trends, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice is achieving best results in addressing affiliate transaction issues faced by both large and medium-sized corporate groups by providing initial assessments, responding to the KFTC’s investigations, and representing clients in related litigation.
  • Regulation of Conglomerates ∙ Holding Companies
    Regulations on corporate groups or holding companies are what Korean corporate groups must always pay close attention to. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust and Competition Practice advises clients on various issues concerning the interpretation and application of the complex legal regime and regulations related to conglomerates or holding companies, and represents clients in Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) investigations and related litigation, based on in-depth legal knowledge and expertise as well as understanding of business needs.
  • Subcontracting
    Subcontracting laws contain many mandatory provisions. This area also requires business sensitivity and knowledge of the relevant industry and the subcontracted work. Based on the collective knowledge and experience of its professionals in this area, Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Compliance Practice counsels clients relating to all aspects of subcontracting, including the analysis of the Korea Fair Trade Commission’s (KFTC) enforcement trends, subcontracting-related compliance, responses to the KFTC’s investigations and deliberations, appeals from the KFTC’s decisions, and follow-up management related to penalty points.
  • Distribution
    The Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) holds a firm regulatory grasp of the distribution sector by dividing it into sub-sectors based on the sub-sector specific laws such as the Fair Transactions in Franchise Business Act, the Act on Fair Transactions in Large Retail Business, the Fair Agency Transactions Act, and the Act on Door-to-Door Sales. Since each of the sub-sectors involves distinct regulatory aspects and risks, they require specialized advice from professionals with expertise and business acumen. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice provides clients with a clear interpretation of those sub-sector specific laws, detailed analysis of decisions rendered by the KFTC and the courts, assessment of business risks involved, and effective management and response measures to minimize potential legal disputes.
  • Terms and Conditions ∙ Labeling and Advertising
    With the rise of online transactions, the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC) is strengthening consumer protection policies. In line with the KFTC’s approach, companies need to identify legal risks in establishig and amending their terms and conditions. Companies also have to respond properly to the KFTC’s investigations, if any, on their terms and conditions. In addition, companies need to monitor whether their labeling/advertising may violate the Act on Fair Labeling and Advertising, while proactively responding to any slanderous advertisements of competitors by filing reports to the KFTC. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice provides clients with legal advice and solutions to all these issues. The Practice also covers the Act on Fair Labeling and Advertising of Foods which has a regulatory regime similar to the Act on Fair Labeling and Advertising.
  • Consumer Protection
    With the rapid development of ICT technologies, various legal issues are being raised in relation to consumer protection laws including the Act on the Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce. Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice provides effective legal advice and creative solutions to address or minimize potential risks and disputes based on its unparalleled experience and expertise in relation to laws and regulations specific to each business sector.
  • Antitrust & Competition Litigation
    Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice represents clients in administrative litigation challenging dispositions rendered by the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC), civil litigation for damages, and criminal litigation resulting from criminal charges filed by the KFTC. The Practice protects and defends interests of our clients in various proceedings based on sophisticated economic analysis and effective presentations.
  • Antitrust & Competition Compliance
    Yoon & Yang’s Antitrust & Competition Practice provides services to ensure compliance with antitrust and competition laws and regulations. Recently, the KFTC has expanded the scope of its on-site investigations to referenced companies and the regulatory authority’s investigation methods based on forensics have also become more advanced. To meet the increasingly complex regulatory landscape, it is becoming more important to ensure antitrust and competition compliance. Compliance services that the Practice offers include designing, implementing and monitoring various compliance programs which meet the standards required by the Korea Fair Trade Commission (KFTC), including regular risk assessment, document management and approval process, in particular regarding affiliate transactions and subcontracting transactions.
